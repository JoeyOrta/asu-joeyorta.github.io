Who are we?

Next Lab sits within Enterprise Technology at ASU where we imagine the future and build ways to help us get there–technology is just one of the tools we use to do this. All of our work is firmly rooted in our core values; we welcome students of all backgrounds and pride ourselves in being a diverse and inclusive environment.

🙌 People to Know 🙌

Our core team consists of 3 full-time staff members and 2 part-time program coordinators. While you report through the Studio team, you may also interface with others on the Core team.

You also work with a large and diverse group of Studio Associates and can familiarize yourself with your colleagues via the Airtable directory.

⚒ Tools and Tech ⚒

We have a few common tools we use to collaborate. It is highly recommended you install each of these free softwares to streamline your work.

It is your responsibility to make sure all work is in the official locations and accessible to your entire team at all times, including your project leads and the Studio Director.

Game Engines - Most of our work relies on either Unity or Unreal game engines. You may also be asked to work in other game engines, like Roblox Studio!

Zoom - Any official Zoom meetings will be scheduled by the Studio Director. It is recommended you use your ASU Zoom account.

Google Drive - Due to file size limitations, Drive is only to be used for collaborative documents (docs, sheets, slides, etc). Shared Drive for NX Studio - Projects and Guilds.

Dropbox - Dropbox is the preferred method for organizing and sharing files and assets.  (Images, 3D models, APKs, zip folders, etc.)
Next Lab Studio Associates on Dropbox

Slack - Your day-to-day communication will be through Slack regarding your projects, and general announcements from the Core team.

asu-next-lab.slack.com is our workspace and you will also be added to the ASU Enterprise Technology workspace. Most channels for projects and guilds in NX are open if you feel like exploring. Look for channels with the prefix “nx-”

Discord 
We cultivate a vibrant guild-based community through our Guilds. All non-internal guild conversations and Unity Upskilling will be done through the Next Lab Community Discord.

Discord is not approved for sensitive data and should not be used for work-related communication. ASU strongly recommends using 2FA to protect your account..

GitHub - All code you create for the Studio will be stored and managed in GitHub.  If you don’t have an account, now is a good time to sign up! Note: 2FA is required to be added to our GitHub organization.

AirTable - This is our primary project management tool in the Studio.  It is used for high-level coordination of projects as well as granular tracking of your individual tasks and assignments.

💻 Types of Work 💻

Projects

Project work accounts for 80% of your weekly time. This entails at least one weekly meeting with your project team(s) and any assigned tasks you were given at the meetings.  If you aren’t sure what to work on, check with your Project Lead.  

You can also work on short-term events/projects hosted by Next Lab.  Watch #nx-support in Slack for these opportunities. There are also administrative tasks under the “Admin Tasks” category on Airtable that anyone can pick up for additional hours!

Guilds/Upskilling

This is your assigned skill area and accounts for 20% of your total weekly hours. That’s right, you get paid to learn! You will spend one hour per week in the Guild meeting and then any additional time is up to you how you spend it.  Occasionally there will be assigned group upskilling where all Guild members complete the same activity.

We participate in the Work+ program at ASU which provides you with upskilling opportunities in eight NACE competencies for career-readiness.  Don’t take this for granted.  You are expected to complete a total of 10 hours of work for this program each semester you are employed.

One resource you can use for your upskilling is the Unity Learn platform. You can earn additional digital credentials for completing Unity Learn pathways in the Next Lab Community Discord server, where you can network and collaborate with other students learning Unity around the world!

If you’re a beginner, start with the Unity Essentials pathway; for those that are more experienced, test out of the Unity Essentials pathway by completing the three mission assessments and try any of the other pathways:
Junior Programmer, Creative Core, VR Development, and Mobile AR Development.

You can also find tutorials, projects, and courses for any particular topic or skill level on the Unity Learn website.

It’s expected that you will work a minimum of 10 hours to a maximum of 20 hours per week. Work fluctuates week by week and sometimes projects will be light. Read the document linked below for information on how and when to log your hours!

How to Log Hours - FAQ & Step-by-Step Guide

The 50% Minimum Rule

You are permitted to work half of your hours remotely, but at LEAST half of your hours must be in person at Next Lab. Working in person allows you to chat with your coworkers, enjoy this awesome work space, and be exposed to spontaneous networking opportunities! Working remotely is a privilege, not an expectation.

Admin shifts

Monday through Friday 8am to 5pm are our standard operating hours and we need one person on hand to help with day-to-day tasks, greet people at the door, and answer any questions for visitors. This is a perfect way to earn extra in-person project hours and familiarize yourself with the people that work here.

Anyone can sign up! Only one person is allowed to work the Admin shift at any given moment, so please do not double up and only sign up if you can fully commit to the entire shift.

There is a document on the Admin table that outlines Admin procedures and responsibilities. Schedule a time with a Program Coordinator to discuss expectations if needed.
